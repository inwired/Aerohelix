Project Overview

Hybrid Wind Energy Solution

Egypt's remote coastal areas — particularly Abu Ramad in Halayeb & Shalateen — face critical energy challenges. Reliance on diesel generators causes pollution, high costs, and frequent outages across approximately 250 housing units.

AER HELIX combines the self-starting torque of Savonius drag-based rotors with the high-efficiency lift generation of Darrieus airfoil blades — creating a system that outperforms either turbine alone.

The 30° helical twist distributes aerodynamic forces along the blade height, reducing torque fluctuations and enabling smoother operation even under turbulent coastal winds.

Real-World Application

Abu Ramad, Halayeb & Shalateen

Abu Ramad is a remote coastal village on Egypt's Red Sea coast facing critical energy infrastructure challenges. With only ~250 housing units and reliance on expensive diesel generators, the community exemplifies the problem AER HELIX is designed to solve.

The region's average wind speed of 6–8 m/s makes it ideal for the hybrid turbine's operational range, enabling reliable low-speed startup and sustained power generation.

AER HELIX could provide electricity for housing, agriculture monitoring, and essential services — replacing diesel and dramatically reducing CO₂ emissions along the Red Sea coast.
